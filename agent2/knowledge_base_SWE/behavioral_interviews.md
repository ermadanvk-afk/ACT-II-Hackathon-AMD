# Behavioral Interviews & Trade-Offs

## The STAR Method
Structure answers concisely:
* Situation: Set the stage.
* Task: Your specific responsibility.
* Action: What *you* did (use "I", not "we").
* Result: Quantified outcome and what you learned.

## Navigating Disagreements & Conflicts
Separating ego from code. Using data/benchmarks to back arguments and committing to decisions ("disagree and commit"). Managing expectations and constructively pushing back against bad ideas.

## Handling Ambiguity, Technical Debt & Trade-offs
Balancing fixing technical debt with shipping features. Proactively communicating risks. Making trade-offs between system constraints (like latency) and model accuracy.

## Failures, Debugging & Accountability
Taking ownership of project failures, missed deadlines, or model degradation in production. Performing blameless post-mortems and putting processes in place (e.g., checking for data drift) to prevent recurrence.
