# Quantitative Foundations Knowledge Base
Tags: math, percentages, mom_growth, yoy_growth, ratios, conversion_funnels, averages, arpu, weighted_mean

## 1. Percentages & Growth Rates
**Concept**: Data Analysts frequently calculate Month-over-Month (MoM) and Year-over-Year (YoY) growth to track business performance.
*   **Formula (Growth Rate)**: `((New Value - Old Value) / Old Value) * 100`
*   **Example Context for AI to generate questions**: E-commerce sales, SaaS active users, app downloads.
*   **Sample Question (Medium)**: "A SaaS company had 4,500 active users in May. In June, they ran a campaign and reached 5,400 users. What was the MoM growth rate?" (Answer: 20%)

## 2. Ratios & Proportions (Conversion Funnels)
**Concept**: Ratios are used to analyze conversion funnels (e.g., how many users who visit a page actually buy).
*   **Formula (Conversion Rate)**: `(Converted Users / Total Visitors) * 100`
*   **Example Context for AI**: Website traffic, marketing ad clicks, cart abandonment.
*   **Sample Question (Hard)**: "An ad generated 10,000 clicks. The click-to-signup ratio is 1:20. Of those who sign up, the signup-to-paid ratio is 2:5. How many paid users were acquired?" (Answer: 200)

## 3. Averages & Weighted Averages
**Concept**: Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) is a standard metric. Weighted averages are used when different segments have different sizes.
*   **Formula (ARPU)**: `Total Revenue / Total Number of Users`
*   **Formula (Weighted Average)**: `(Sum of (Value * Weight)) / (Sum of Weights)`
*   **Example Context for AI**: Subscription tiers (Basic, Pro, Enterprise), regional sales data.
*   **Sample Question (Hard)**: "A company has 1,000 'Basic' users paying $10/month, and 200 'Pro' users paying $50/month. What is the overall ARPU?" (Answer: $16.67)
