## 1. The STAR Framework
**Concept**: The standard framework for answering behavioral interview questions.
*   **S - Situation**: Set the scene and give necessary context.
*   **T - Task**: Describe what your responsibility was in that situation.
*   **A - Action**: Explain exactly what steps *you* took to address it.
*   **R - Result**: Share the outcomes your actions achieved (must be quantified if possible).
*   **AI Interviewer Instruction**: If the candidate fails to quantify the 'Result' or is too vague about the 'Action', the AI must ask follow-up questions like: "Can you specify what *your* exact role was in that project?" or "What was the measurable impact of that action?"

## 2. Stakeholder Management (Non-Technical Audiences)
**Concept**: Data Analysts must explain complex metrics to people who don't know math/SQL.
*   **Core Trait**: Empathy, avoiding jargon, using analogies.
*   **Scenario for AI to Roleplay**: Act as a Marketing Manager who is confused why the "Bounce Rate" went up and thinks it's a good thing. Ask the candidate to explain it simply.

## 3. Handling Conflict & Pushback
**Concept**: DAs often get requests for data that are impossible, flawed, or not aligned with business goals.
*   **Core Trait**: Prioritization, diplomatic pushback, suggesting alternatives.
*   **Scenario for AI to Roleplay**: Act as a demanding Sales VP who wants a complex machine learning dashboard built by tomorrow. The candidate must successfully de-escalate and propose a simpler MVP (Minimum Viable Product).
